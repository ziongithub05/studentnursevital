Cardiology

Definition

Hypertension is a common condition characterized by elevated blood pressure in the arteries. Blood pressure is the force exerted by the blood against the walls of the arteries as the heart pumps it around the body. High blood pressure puts extra strain on the heart and blood vessels, increasing the risk of serious health problems, including:

Complications caused by Hypertension

Heart Disease:

High blood pressure can damage the arteries, making them more susceptible to the buildup of plaque and narrowing (atherosclerosis). This increases the risk of coronary artery disease, heart attack, and other cardiovascular conditions.

Stroke:

High blood pressure can weaken and damage the blood vessels in the brain, increasing the risk of stroke. A stroke occurs when blood flow to part of the brain is blocked or reduced, depriving brain cells of oxygen and nutrients.

Kidney Disease:

High blood pressure can damage the blood vessels in the kidneys, reducing their ability to function properly. Over time, this can lead to kidney disease or kidney failure.

Nursing Managements

Regularly measure and monitor blood pressure levels, educating patients about the importance of blood pressure control and tracking readings at home.

Administer antihypertensive medications as prescribed, educate patients about their medications, and monitor for side effects or adverse reactions.

Educate patients about lifestyle changes to help lower blood pressure, including dietary modifications (e.g., reducing sodium intake, following the DASH diet), increasing physical activity, maintaining a healthy weight, limiting alcohol consumption, and managing stress.

Provide comprehensive education about hypertension, its risk factors, complications, and the importance of adherence to treatment plans, including medication adherence and regular follow-up appointments.

Monitor patients' response to treatment, assess for complications such as target organ damage (e.g., heart, kidneys, eyes), and collaborate with other healthcare providers to adjust treatment plans as needed.
